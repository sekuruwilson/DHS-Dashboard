Methodology

The RDHS 2019–20 used a two-stage stratified cluster sampling design, ensuring representativeness at the national, provincial, and district levels.

14,784 households selected across all 30 districts

Stratified by urban/rural residence

Women aged 15–49 and men aged 15–54 interviewed

Plus child-level biomarker data collected

CAPI (Computer-Assisted Personal Interviewing)

Digital data collection for higher accuracy

Biomarker testing — anemia, malaria, HIV & more

Blood and anthropometric measurements taken

Survey History

Rwanda has conducted six rounds of the DHS since 1992, each building on the last.

{% for event in timeline|default:"" %} {% empty %}
RDHS VI — 2019–2020

Most recent edition. Digital (CAPI) data collection across all 30 districts. HIV testing, malaria biomarkers, and disability module included.

RDHS V — 2014–2015

Expanded malaria testing and nutrition surveillance. Established Rwanda as a model for DHS implementation in Sub-Saharan Africa.

RDHS IV — 2010

First district-level disaggregated reporting. Strengthened household wealth index and water/sanitation modules.

RDHS III — 2005

Post-genocide reconstruction era. First round to include HIV prevalence testing at national scale.

RDHS II — 2000

Re-established national health baselines following the 1994 crisis. Focused on fertility and child mortality.

RDHS I — 1992

First national DHS in Rwanda. Established foundational demographic baselines for the country.

About This Data Portal

This portal was developed by the National Institute of Statistics Rwanda (NISR) to make RDHS 2019–20 findings accessible to policymakers, researchers, and the public.

All data is disaggregated at the district level and covers key domains including household characteristics, health outcomes, nutrition, reproductive health, child mortality, and wealth. Use the dashboard to explore indicators interactively, and the Analytics section to compare districts and identify gaps.
